A 2017 study in the Journal of Strength and Conditioning Research found that bodyweight exercises produce similar strength gains to gym-based exercises for beginners and intermediate trainees. The key is progressive overload — making exercises harder over time. Your body weight, gravity, and consistency are all you need.

Warm-up (5 min)

  • Jumping jacks (60 sec)
  • Arm circles (30 sec)
  • High knees (60 sec)
  • Bodyweight squats (15 reps)
  • Inchworms (8 reps)

Circuit 1 (8 min, 40 sec work / 20 sec rest)

  • Push-ups → Bodyweight squats → Reverse lunges → Plank shoulder taps → Glute bridges

Rest 60 seconds.

Circuit 2 (8 min)

  • Diamond push-ups → Jump squats → Walking lunges → Mountain climbers → Superman holds

Rest 60 seconds.

Circuit 3 (8 min)

  • Wide push-ups → Bulgarian split squats → Side lunges → Plank up-downs → Single-leg deadlifts

Cool-down (3 min)

  • Standing quad stretch → Hamstring stretch → Chest opener → Child's pose

Exercise Progressions

📈 Push-up Progression

  • Standard push-ups
  • Elevate feet (decline)
  • Archer push-ups
  • One-arm push-up

📈 Squat Progression

  • Bodyweight squats
  • Jump squats
  • Pistol squat progression
  • Shrimp squats

Weekly Schedule

DayActivity
MondayFull body (this routine)
TuesdayActive recovery (30-min walk + stretching)
WednesdayFull body (increase reps by 10%)
ThursdayRest or yoga
FridayFull body (try harder progressions)
SaturdayCardio (run, bike, or jump rope for 20-30 min)
SundayComplete rest
💡 Key Principle: Consistency matters more than intensity. Track your reps and times weekly. The moment an exercise feels easy for 3 consecutive sessions, progress to the harder variation. Three months of this routine will transform your fitness without stepping foot in a gym.
